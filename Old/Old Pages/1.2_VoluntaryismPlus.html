Voluntaryism, Plus

Conceptually, Voluntaryism is a very simple moral philosophy – it is the basic proposition that all human interaction should be directly consensual. Voluntaryism rejects the initiation of force in all its various forms including physical violence, threats of violence, theft, bullying, slavery, rape, murder, etc. However, unlike Pacifism, Voluntaryism does not bar the victim of coercion from responding in a strictly self-defensive manner.

- Peter Miller, Introduction to Voluntaryism

Intro to Voluntaryism

Voluntaryism is the moral philosophy that all forms of interaction should be voluntary, and that the only just use of force is defensive force.

We abide by the Non-Aggression Principle (NAP) which dictates that no action be taken that violates another’s consent if not in self-defense.

Voluntaryism means only consensual interactions between individuals. It also implies that there are no rights that can be granted to an individual that aren’t shared by all individuals - such as any rights we grant the “government” to rule over our lives and livelihoods. Rights can neither be granted nor revoked. They are either respected or not.

Ultimately no one thinks that anyone should need to rule over them - especially someone that they didn’t choose themselves - which means no one really supports involuntary rulership over others. And yet due to a lack of options as well as various methods of social and psychological conditioning, many people think that some groups possess the right to intimidate and initiate force against others and enslave them.
